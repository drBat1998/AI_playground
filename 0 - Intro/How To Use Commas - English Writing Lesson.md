# Notes
1. The listing comma
	the listing comma replaces words like `and` and `or`	
	She has long, dark, shiny hair - She has long and dark and shiny hair
	He bought a bottle of dark German beer - He bought a bottle of dark _and_ German beer
2. The joining comma is used to join two complete sentences, together with a linking word.
	 `We were tired`, and `we didn't feel like going anywhere`.
3. The bracketing comma is used to add extra information about
	This book, first published in 1956, is still useful for students today.
	Similar to most people his age, he isn't really thinking about his future. 

Common mistakes 
1. Don't put a comma between a subject and its verb.
2. Don't use a comma before `that`.
3. Don't use a comma to join two sentences if you don't use a linking word.
