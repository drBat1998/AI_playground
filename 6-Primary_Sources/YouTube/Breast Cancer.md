# Notes
Normal -> Atypical hyperplasia -> Carcinoma in situ -> Invasive carcinoma

Non-invasive breast cancers:
1. DCIS -> Asymptomatic with micro-calification -> high risk for ipsilateral carcinoma.
2. Pagets diesease ->DCIS with spreading to nipple -> Nipple eczema and bloody nipple discharge.
3. Lobular carcinoma in situ -> asymptomatic with susp. fibroadenoma -> bilateral carcinoma risk.

Invasive breast cancers
1. IDC(70) -> palpable mass in the upper outer quadrant.
2. ILC -> asymptomatic, found in mammography.
3. Inflammatory breast cancer -> edema and commonly bilateral. 

Risk factors:
1. Estrogen exposure
	1. Ovarian estrogen 
	2. adipose estrogen 
	3. exogene estrogen
2. Decrease tumor suppressor gene activity
	1. BRACA 1 / BRACA 2
	2. Tp53 - Fanuren syndr
