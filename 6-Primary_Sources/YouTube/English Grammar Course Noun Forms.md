# Notes
# 1.1 Forms and functions
What is a noun?
- Nouns represent the people, places, things, concepts, and more in sentences. 

Single word: 
- one noun -> general, abstract way
	Humans crave freedom. 
	Humanity craves freedom. 

Noun groups
noun with complementing modifiers -> more specific, limits, or expands.

Noun groups consist of a "head" noun and modifiers. Modifiers, compared to the "head" noun, do not directly affect other parts of speech in the sentences. 

Nount + noun
1. Compounds (two or more nouns working as one) - basketball, son-in-law
2. Nouns acting as adjectives to other nouns - city boy, sports car, a clothes store

Noun's  + noun(s) - my sister's dog, Craig's book. 
e.g a man's leg vs. a table leg

Noun + preposition + noun - the book on the shelf, the girl next door, an excuse for the delay. 

Noun + other complements 
- prepositional phrase - no hope of winning
- infinitive - a desire to help
- clause - the belef that you are right
