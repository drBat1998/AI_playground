# Notes
essential and non-essential information
- essential - need
-  non-essential - the information can been removed

The woman ==which working in the bakery== is my sister. (esential ditail, without comma)
My sister==, who works in the bakery,== is very kind. (extra diteil, with comma)

Who - about people 
1. essential information 
	Girl who unswer the phone was very polite. 
	The employee who manage the account is on holliday.
2. non-essential
	My friend Tom, who lives in New Yourk, is visiting next week. 
	Sarah makes this cake -> Sarah, who loves baking, makes this cake.

If who refers to the subject you need it. If who refers to the object you may leave it. 

WHICH - things and animals
1. essential information 
	The book is amazing (which book) -> The book which won the prize is amazing.
	The dog kept us awake (which dog) -> The dog which barked all night kept us awake.
2. non-essential
	 This website is very helpful -> This website, which I use daily, is very helpful. 
The chair (which) she painted looks new. -> we can left which if it is essential

if the information we add essential we must use which -> The novel, which I read last summer, is a classic. 

THAT - for people and things only for essential information
The teacher that/who helped me was kind. 
The jacket that/which lent me fits perfectly.

We can delete it if it is the object and not subject.
The coat (that) I borrowed is in the wardrobe.

WHOSE - posesion
1. essential information 
	The woman whose phone rang during the interview was embarrassed.
	The artist whose paintings were stolen is devastated.
	
2. non-essential
	 My cousin, whose French is excellent, moved to France.
	 Mr. Allen, whose office is next to mine, is retiring soon.

WHERE - places
1. essential information 
	The school where she teaches is very moderm.	
2. non-essential
	 Hyde Park, where many concerts are held, is beautiful in spring. 

That's the school where I studied -> That's the school I studied at.

WHEN - time
1. essential information 
	The moment when I proposed will stay with me forever. 
2. non-essential
	His birthday, when we all gathered together, was unforgettable. 

The year (when) we moved was a busy year.
