> PANoptosis plays a crucial role in several diseases, including infectious diseases, autoinflammatory diseases, and cancer.13–18 The understanding and manipulation of this pathway could potentially lead to new therapeutic strategies for these diseases. However, when PANoptosis is activated, blocking just one arm (i.e., blocking just pyroptosis or apoptosis or necroptosis alone) cannot prevent cell death or inflammatory cytokine‐induced diseases.16,19 Several studies have suggested that the inflammasome/pyroptosis (caspase‐1) may be a critical component of the protein complex that initiates PANoptosis, the PANoptosome.10,20
