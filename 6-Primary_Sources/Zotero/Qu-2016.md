> [!meta]- Metadata
> abstract:: Mixed lineage kinase domain-like protein (MLKL) is a critical molecule mediating cell necroptosis. However, its role in brain injury remains obscure. We first  investigated the functions and mechanisms of MLKL in mediating neuronal damage in  developing brain after hypoxia-ischemia. Neuronal necroptosis was induced by  oxygen-glucose deprivation (OGD) plus caspase inhibitor zVAD treatment  (OGD/zVAD). We found that two important necroptosis related proteins,  receptor-interacting protein 1 and 3 (RIP1, RIP3) were upregulated. Furthermore,  the interaction of RIP1-RIP3 with MLKL increased. Inhibition of MLKL through  siRNA diminished RIP1-RIP3-MLKL interaction and attenuated neuronal death induced  by OGD/zVAD. The translocation of oligomerized MLKL to the neuronal membrane  leading to the injury of cellular membrane is the possible new mechanism of  neuronal necroptosis. Animal experiment with neonatal rats further proved that  MLKL inhibition attenuated brain damage induced by hypoxia-ischemia. These  findings suggest that MLKL is a target to attenuate brain damage in developing  brain.
