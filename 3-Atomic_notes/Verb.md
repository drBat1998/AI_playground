# Notes
Verbs are words that describe actions or states of being.

Types of verbs:
1. dynamic or action
	1. physical action  - walk, swim, play.
	2. mental or internal actions - guess, consider, endure. 
2. Stative verbs - state or feeling - want, love, hate, know.
3. Verbs that can be dynamic or stative - see, hear, taste, smell, feel. 
	1. When perception verbs are used as involuntary action, such as passive or unintentional actions, they are stative. I can't see without my glasses. Cake still tastes great.
	2. If the verbs are used as voluntary action, specific, deliberate, and temporary events, they are dynamic. I haven't been seeing well since I lost my glasses. We were tasting cakes for the wedding all afternoon.
	3. If the perception verbs are part of phrasal verbs or expressions. 
_I think toads are better than frogs._ 
(stative: expresses an opinion or feeling always there; involuntary)
_All morning I was thinking about how toads are better than frogs._ 
(dynamic: expresses the temporary action of thinking; voluntary)

_I have a ten-year-old dog._ 
(stative: expresses permanent ownership; involuntary)
_I am having a party for my dog’s eleventh birthday._ 
(dynamic: used as part of phrase; voluntary)

_He is nice to everyone._ 
(stative: expresses an ongoing state or personality trait; involuntary)
_He was just being nice to everyone to get a promotion._ 
(dynamic: expresses a temporary/intentional state; voluntary)

Auxiliary verbs
- used to change another verb's tense, voice, or mood. - be, have, and do. 
- Some auxiliary verbs are added to another verb to show necessity, possibility, or capability. They called the modal auxiliary verbs. - can, may, could, might. 

Phrasal verbs 
- are phrases that act as individual verbs. 

Verb categories
- Verbs with direct and indirect objects
	1. Intransitive - complete action without need in direct or indirect object - go, walk, run, talk, sit, work.
	2. Transitive - use direct, but not an indirect object - clean, like, love, dislike, hate
	3. Ditransitive - use both direct and indirect objects - throw, make, buy, sell, give, bring.
- active vs passive
- Linking or copular verbs are any verb, dynamic or stative, that directly connects or “links” the sentence’s subject to other words in the sentence. - be, become, seem

Verb forms
	root is the basic form of the verb.
	third-person singular present -  sue with subjects like he, she, it, singular they. - tries, says, kisses, 
	simple past - shows an action that already happened
	present participle - show ongoing or current action
	past participle -  is used for the perfect tenses
