# Notes
A noun is a word that names something.
Types of nouns
1. Proper nouns - specific names with capitalization.
2. Common nouns - general names with lowercase. 
	1. Concrete nouns - something tangible and physical - doorbell and keyboard
	2. Abstract nouns - something that cannot be perceived by the senses. - Courage, love.
	3. Collective nouns - denote a group - pack or pride. - Collective nouns are accompanied by singular verbs
Countable and uncountable
Uncountable 
- intangible concepts - information, wealth.
- collections of things that are considered as wholes - jewelry, equipment, the working class.
- homogeneous physical substance - milk, sand, air. 
Singular uncountable nouns can be expressed in countable units by adding a countable noun - a piece of, some

Singular vs. plural

Gerunds - verbs acting as nouns - Swimming is fun. 

#### Functions of nouns
Nouns as subjects
Nouns as objects
- direct object - a noun that receives the action described by the verb
- indirect object - a noun that receives a direct object
          d.o.           i.o.
Ivan sent _Stephan_ the _letter_.

Nouns as subject complement
- Give information about the subject.
- connected by a verb such as be, become, or seem.
Nouns as object complement
- Give information about the direct object.
Nouns as appositives
- noun to further define or identify it.
- can be restrictive or non-restrictive.
- _My brother,_ **_Michael_**_, is six years old._ where _Michael_ is an appositive here.
Nouns as modifiers
- function as adjectives
- He is a speed demon.
