# Notes
The gut microbiome has been shown to have a significant role in the well-being of any human being. The influence can be broadly categorized into four more or less distinct pathways: neuronal, endocrine, immune, and metabolic.  

Here, Liu et al try to classify of main components of the gut metabolome by dividing it into: 
1. Short-chain fatty acids, such as acetate, propionate, butyrate, hexanoate, isovalerate, isobutyrate, 2-methylpropionate, directly act on GPR receptors and HDAC 1/3.
2. Bile acid derivatives, such as cholate, hyocholate, deoxycholate, taurohyocholate, ursodeoxycholate, that directly act on  FXR, VDR, PXR/SXR, CAR, TGR5, S1PR2, FPR, mAChR receptors. 
3. Tryptophan and indole derivatives, such as indole-3-lactic acid, indole acetic acid, indole-3-acetamide, indole pyruvic acid, indoxyl sulfuric acid, indole, and serotonin, targeting AhR and PXR receptors. 
4. Choline metabolites, such as TMA, methylamine, dimethylglycine, and dimethylamine, by an unknown mechanism activate NF-ÐºB, protein kinase C, and NLRP3 inflammasome.
5. Vitamins, such as B2, B3, B5, B6, B9, B12, and K, by and vitamin receptors. 
6. Neurotransmitters, such as dopamine, catecholamines, 5-HT, and GABA, act through their receptors. 
Liu J, Tan Y, Cheng H, Zhang D, Feng W, Peng C. Functions of Gut Microbiota Metabolites, Current Status and Future Perspectives. Aging Dis. 2022 Jul 11;13(4):1106-1126. doi: 10.14336/AD.2022.0104. PMID: 35855347; PMCID: PMC9286904.
